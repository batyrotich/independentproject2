Akan names are derived from Ghanian culture. Children are given their first name as a 'day name' which corresponds to the day in the week they were born.

The naming ceremony is carried out at the fathers house on the eight day, a week after the day of child birth.

Why wait for seven days? This is to ascertsin that the child has come to stay on earth and will not return prematurely to the ancestors.
